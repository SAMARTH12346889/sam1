IPC

The Indian Penal Code (IPC) is the main criminal code in India, defining various criminal offenses and their penalties. Enacted in 1860, it covers a wide range of crimes, including theft, murder, assault, and fraud. The IPC serves as a legal framework to maintain law and order in society by prescribing punishments for criminal acts. It is applicable across India, except in areas governed by special laws.
